# Traveling with Kids

Trip 1 starts Feb 2027. Baby born late 2026 = ~4 months old at departure.

Babies this age are actually ideal travel companions â€” they sleep most of the day, breastfeed on demand, and don't need entertainment. No tantrums, no "are we there yet," no screen time negotiations. They're portable, curious, and adaptable. The first year is arguably the easiest window for travel before toddler mobility kicks in.

Plenty of overlanding families start younger. The rhythm of the road â€” motion, white noise, new stimulation â€” often suits babies better than a static apartment.

## Practical Notes

- **Driving rhythm:** 2-hour blocks with breaks work for everyone, not just baby
- **Accommodation mix:** More hotels in Trips 1-3 for easier nights, shift to camping as they grow
- **Car seat:** Rear-facing Nuna Pipa RX with ISOFIX (LC300 equipped)
- **Rough roads:** Air down tires, drive slower on corrugated sections â€” standard overlanding practice anyway
