New Zealand's biosecurity is among the world's strictest‚Äîensure the vehicle is spotless before shipping from Townsville. Auckland spreads across an isthmus between two harbors, its skyline dominated by the Sky Tower. One-third of New Zealand's population lives here, yet within an hour you're in genuine wilderness.

Morning devoted to vehicle clearance‚ÄîMPI (Ministry for Primary Industries) inspects all vehicles for biological risks. Once cleared, explore Auckland: the Sky Tower for 360-degree views, Viaduct Harbour's restaurants, the volcanic cone of Mount Eden for city panoramas. New Zealand awaits.

The Bay of Islands is where New Zealand's founding document was signed in 1840‚Äîthe Treaty of Waitangi between Maori chiefs and the British Crown. The bay's 144 islands dot azure waters; Russell, the first capital, was once known as "the Hellhole of the Pacific."

Short drive to Paihia, the Bay of Islands' main town. Afternoon at the Waitangi Treaty Grounds‚Äîthe carved meeting house, war canoe, and interpretive center explain the complex history. Ferry to Russell for a wander through New Zealand's oldest stone building (Pompallier Mission) and oldest pub (Duke of Marlborough).

## Week 2: Cape Reinga & Kauri Coast

Cape Reinga is New Zealand's northernmost point accessible by road and deeply sacred to Maori‚ÄîTe Rerenga Wairua means "the leaping-off place of spirits." Here, the Tasman Sea and Pacific Ocean meet in visible turbulence. The iconic lighthouse marks where spirits depart for the afterlife.

Early departure for the cape. The route offers two options: sealed road out, 90 Mile Beach (actually 88 km) return‚Äîthe beach is an official highway, drivable at low tide with care. At Cape Reinga, walk to the lighthouse for views of the meeting oceans. The 800-year-old pohutukawa tree clings to the cliff‚Äîthrough it, spirits descend to the sea.

The Footprint of the Kauri walk explains the forest ecology. Strict biosecurity: clean shoes at every entrance to protect against kauri dieback disease.

The East Cape lighthouse (21 km from Te Araroa, then 700 steps up) marks the easternmost point of mainland New Zealand‚Äîthe first place on Earth to see each day's sunrise.

Very early departure (around 4am) to reach the lighthouse for sunrise. The 700 steps are challenging but rewarding‚Äîwatching the sun rise over the Pacific from New Zealand's easternmost point is unforgettable. Return to Te Araroa for breakfast and rest.
